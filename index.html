Short Bio

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Economics at UCLA Anderson School of Management, working under the supervision of Prof. Paola Giuliano and Prof. Nico Voigtländer.

My research sits at the intersection of economic history and political economy. I study the economic origins and consequences of conflict, religion, and public policy, with a focus on long-run development, human capital, and institutional change.


Research Interests

  • Secondary: Public Economics, Labor Economics.
